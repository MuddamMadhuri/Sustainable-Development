Sustainable Development Goals

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, unanimously adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, serves as a collective roadmap towards global peace, prosperity, and sustainability. Central to this agenda are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which demand immediate action from all nations, both developed and developing, through international collaboration. These goals underscore the interconnectedness of poverty eradication, enhanced healthcare and education, reduced inequality, and economic advancement, alongside concerted efforts to combat climate change and safeguard our natural ecosystems, including oceans and forests.

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are the culmination of years of collaborative efforts between countries and the United Nations, including the UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs. This journey traces back to the Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro in June 1992, where Agenda 21 was embraced by over 178 nations, outlining a global strategy for sustainable development to enhance human well-being and safeguard the environment.
  1. Building on this momentum, the Millennium Summit in September 2000 led to the adoption of the Millennium Declaration, resulting in the formulation of the eight Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) aimed at alleviating extreme poverty by 2015. Subsequently, the World Summit on Sustainable Development in Johannesburg in 2002 reinforced commitments to poverty eradication and environmental protection, expanding upon previous frameworks such as Agenda 21 and the Millennium Declaration by emphasizing the importance of multilateral partnerships.

  2. The United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development (Rio+20) in June 2012 marked another pivotal moment, resulting in the adoption of "The Future We Want," which initiated the process of crafting the SDGs to succeed the MDGs. This event also ushered in the establishment of the UN High-level Political Forum on Sustainable Development and outlined further measures for sustainable development implementation.

  3. In 2013, the General Assembly established an Open Working Group to draft a proposal for the SDGs, leading to the initiation of negotiations on the post-2015 development agenda in January 2015. This process culminated in the historic adoption of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, comprising 17 SDGs, during the UN Sustainable Development Summit in September 2015.

  4. 2015 witnessed several landmark agreements, including the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction, the Addis Ababa Action Agenda on Financing for Development, the Paris Agreement on Climate Change, and the transformative 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

  5. Today, the annual High-level Political Forum on Sustainable Development serves as the primary UN platform for monitoring and reviewing progress towards achieving the SDGs, symbolizing the ongoing commitment to global sustainable development.
